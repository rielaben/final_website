History

This page delves into the history of our club!

Founding

In 2016, Rohit Mogalayapalli founded the Michigan Sports Analytics Society as a University of Michigan sophomore. The club started with analyzing professional sports datasets from sites like Basketball Reference as well as conducting analytics work for University of Michigan varsity sports teams.

Baltimore Ravens Free Agency Competition

In 2017, we partnered with the Baltimore Ravens to hold a contest where students were tasked with building an analytical model to accurately predict the salaries of the 2018 NFL Free Agent Class.

2017 MSAS Hackathon

In 2017, we hosted our first hackathon! The MSAS Hackathon was an event designed to increase knowledge of both our group and the field of sports analytics at the University of Michigan. The event drew a wide range of students and faculty, and gave participants the opportunity to get hands-on practice in the field of analytics.

2018 Michigan Sports Analytics Symposium

In 2018 we created The Michigan Sports Analytics Symposium. This event was designed to showcase the field of sports analytics at the University of Michigan and beyond, as well as cultivate interest in the future of the field. The Symposium included presentations from both students and faculty, who shared various projects and findings from the field of sports analytics with the audience. The Symposium was a huge success, and it laid the groundwork for MSAS symposiums in the future.

2019

In 2019, we had a fantastic year, running successful 2019 Hackathon and 2019 Symposium events. Our attendance grew, and our founder, Rohit, accepted a position to work for the Baltimore Ravens as a Performance Analytics Intern!

2020

2020, like for many organizations, was a tough year. We unfortunately had to cancel the 2020 Michigan Sports Analytics Symposium due to covid, and after a great school year of progress the club operations came to a screeching halt in March with the lockdowns. After months of preparation from the leadership team, the club underwent a successful transition to our new, virtual, format for the 2020-2021 school year. Even with the virtual format we had a ton of fun running meetings and learning more about sports analytics!

2021

2021 brings new opportunities for the organization. In April, we will be hosting the first ever virtual Michigan Sports Analytics Symposium!
